Day 1 • Hip (4 exercises) + Shoulder (3 exercises)

Resisted Bridge (band above knees)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set, 5‑sec hold at top (confirm with your physio if they intended more sets). Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Band just above knees; feet hip‑width; ribs down; pelvis neutral.

Start (what to think)

  • Light tension into band (knees gently out). Brace core like you’re about to be tapped in the stomach.

Move (direction & control)

  • Push through heels; lift hips until shoulders‑hips‑knees line up.
  • Keep knees tracking over mid‑foot (don’t let them cave in).

Peak / End range

  • Squeeze glutes hard for 5 seconds; keep lower back relaxed (no arching).

Where you should feel it

  • Glutes + hamstrings. You should NOT feel pinching at the front of the hip or low‑back dominance.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Over‑arching the low back at the top.
  • Feet too far away (hamstrings cramp) or too close (quads dominate).
  • Knees collapsing in despite band.

Resisted Hip Extension + External Rotation (prone)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set (as per physio email). Focus: controlled reps

Video

  • No single perfect embed for this one — use the “Open video” buttons below.

Setup

  • Lie on stomach; legs straight; band around knees/thighs as prescribed.
  • Keep pelvis heavy on the floor; gently brace abdomen.

Start (what to think)

  • Point toes up slightly (dorsiflex), rotate leg outward slightly (from hip), keep knee straight.

Move (direction & control)

  • Lift leg a few inches using glute (not low back).
  • Move slowly: 2 sec up, 2 sec down.

Peak / End range

  • Stop before your pelvis tips or your low back arches.
  • Think: “long leg, small lift.”

Where you should feel it

  • Glute max/hamstring + deep hip rotators.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Lifting too high and arching the back.
  • Turning the whole body/pelvis instead of rotating at the hip.

Resisted Hip Internal Rotation (prone, knees bent)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Lie on stomach; bend knees to ~90°.
  • Band around ankles (or as prescribed).
  • Hips stay square to the floor.

Start (what to think)

  • Gently brace core; keep thighs on the floor.

Move (direction & control)

  • Rotate the working foot outward (so the hip internally rotates).
  • Slow and controlled — avoid momentum.

Peak / End range

  • Pause 1–2 sec at end range without pelvis rolling.

Where you should feel it

  • Front/side of hip (TFL/deep rotators) and control muscles.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Pelvis rolling open.
  • Knee drifting away; movement coming from lumbar spine.

Resisted Straight Leg Raise (SLR)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Lie on back; one knee bent, other leg straight.
  • Band at ankles if prescribed; keep toes pulled up.

Start (what to think)

  • Lock knee straight by tightening thigh (quad).

Move (direction & control)

  • Lift straight leg to ~30–45° (or comfortable) without bending knee.
  • Lower slowly without letting heel slam.

Peak / End range

  • Pause briefly; keep pelvis still (no hiking).

Where you should feel it

  • Front thigh (quad) + hip flexor; mild effort in core.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Knee bending.
  • Lower back arching; ribs flaring.

Scapular Retraction (band around wrists)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set, 5‑sec hold. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Stand tall; band looped around wrists; elbows soft.
  • Neck long; ribs stacked over pelvis.

Start (what to think)

  • Let shoulders relax down (no shrug).

Move (direction & control)

  • Pull shoulder blades “back and down” and hold.
  • Keep chin gently tucked; don’t flare ribs.

Peak / End range

  • Feel a firm squeeze between shoulder blades for 5 sec.

Where you should feel it

  • Mid/lower traps + rhomboids. Should not pinch the front of shoulder.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Shrugging up.
  • Over‑arching low back to ‘cheat’ the squeeze.

Shoulder Flexion with External Rotation (band around wrists)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Loop light band around wrists; elbows close to sides to start.
  • Hands slightly apart to create light outward tension.

Start (what to think)

  • Set shoulder blades gently down and back; ribs down.

Move (direction & control)

  • Maintain band tension (wrists trying to spread) while raising arms.
  • Move within pain‑free range; keep neck relaxed.

Peak / End range

  • At top, you should still feel outward tension (ER) — no wrist collapse.

Where you should feel it

  • Rotator cuff + upper back control; not upper traps.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Shrugging.
  • Losing band tension as arms go up.
  • Flaring ribs/arching back.

Banded Wall Clock / Scapular Clock

Dose: 5 reps × 1 set. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Band around hands; stand facing wall.
  • Hands on wall at shoulder height; gentle band tension.

Start (what to think)

  • Shoulder blades ‘set’ (back/down) and ribs down.

Move (direction & control)

  • Slide one hand to different ‘clock’ positions while the other stabilizes.
  • Go slow and keep trunk still.

Peak / End range

  • At farthest reach: keep shoulder blade controlled (no shrug/winging).

Where you should feel it

  • Serratus + rotator cuff + mid back control.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Shrugging or winging shoulder blade.
  • Twisting body to reach.

Resisted Hip Abduction (side‑lying, band at ankles)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Lie on side; bottom knee slightly bent; top leg straight.
  • Band at ankles; core gently braced.

Start (what to think)

  • Top leg in line with body (don’t drift forward). Toes slightly down.

Move (direction & control)

  • Lift top leg straight up slowly; keep pelvis stacked.
  • Lower with control.

Peak / End range

  • Stop before pelvis rolls back; pause briefly.

Where you should feel it

  • Outer hip/glute med (side of butt).

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Hip rolling open.
  • Leg drifting forward (hits hip flexor/TFL).

Resisted Clams

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Side‑lying; hips stacked; knees bent; band above knees/thighs.

Start (what to think)

  • Feet together; slight forward tilt of pelvis (ribs down).

Move (direction & control)

  • Open top knee without rolling hips back.
  • Close slowly.

Peak / End range

  • Pause briefly; keep feet together.

Where you should feel it

  • Outer glute (not front of hip).

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Rolling pelvis back.
  • Letting feet separate.

Single‑Leg Calf Raise (on wobble cushion/foam)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set each side. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Stand on pad; light finger support on a rail if needed.
  • Toes forward; tripod foot (big toe, little toe, heel).

Start (what to think)

  • Lock knee softly (not hyperextended).

Move (direction & control)

  • Rise up slowly; pause; lower for 2–3 seconds.

Peak / End range

  • At top: stay tall, don’t let ankle roll out.

Where you should feel it

  • Calf + foot/ankle stabilizers.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Bouncing.
  • Letting arch collapse or ankle roll.

Single‑Leg Hinge (Hip Hinge / Single‑Leg RDL pattern)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Stand tall; soft knee bend on stance leg.
  • Hips square; hands on hips if helpful.

Start (what to think)

  • Brace core; keep back long (neutral).

Move (direction & control)

  • Hinge from hip: chest and back leg move together.
  • Stop when you feel hamstring tension; return by squeezing glute.

Peak / End range

  • At bottom: hips square; no twisting; keep balance.

Where you should feel it

  • Hamstring + glute on stance leg.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Rounding back.
  • Hips opening/rotating out.
  • Knee collapsing inward.

Lunge on Unstable Surface (balance pad / wobble cushion)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Pad in front; step onto center of pad.
  • Torso tall; front foot tripod.

Start (what to think)

  • Brace core; pelvis level.

Move (direction & control)

  • Lower straight down; knee tracks over 2nd–3rd toe.
  • Push through front foot to rise.

Peak / End range

  • Top: regain balance; avoid wobbling knee inward.

Where you should feel it

  • Glute/quads + ankle control.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Knee caving in.
  • Leaning forward excessively.
▶ Open video: Physitrack: Lunging onto a balance mat (video)

Forearm Plank to Push‑Up Position (Plank Up‑Down)

Dose: 30 seconds × 1 set (as prescribed). Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Forearm plank: elbows under shoulders; glutes gently on; ribs down.

Start (what to think)

  • Make body a straight line head‑to‑heels.

Move (direction & control)

  • Press up one arm at a time to high plank; lower one arm at a time.
  • Keep hips level (no twisting).

Peak / End range

  • High plank: hands under shoulders; shoulder blades stable.

Where you should feel it

  • Core anti‑rotation + shoulder stability.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Hips rocking side to side.
  • Shrugging shoulders.

Hip Rotation Mobility (kneeling drill)

Dose: 10 reps × 1 set. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Kneel on a pillow for comfort.
  • Keep pelvis square; gentle brace.

Start (what to think)

  • Move only through the hip (not the low back).

Move (direction & control)

  • Slowly rotate through the range you own; breathe steadily.

Peak / End range

  • Stop at first strong stretch; no pinching.

Where you should feel it

  • Deep hip capsule stretch/mobility.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Forcing range; compensating with low back.

Hip Mobility Combo (Adductors → Hip Flexors → Pigeon)

Dose: 3 reps × 1 set, 30‑sec hold each position. Focus: controlled reps

Video

  • No single perfect embed for this one — use the “Open video” buttons below.

Setup

  • Move slowly between positions; keep breathing nasal and calm.

Start (what to think)

  • Adductor stretch: side lunge, extended leg straight, toes forward.

Move (direction & control)

  • Transition: turn into hip‑flexor/prisoner stretch; squeeze back‑leg glute.
  • Then move into pigeon; hips square as able.

Peak / End range

  • In each hold: exhale and let tension drop; no sharp pain.

Where you should feel it

  • Inner thigh → front of hip → glute/buttock stretch.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Letting low back take over in hip flexor stretch.
  • Twisting in pigeon; forcing knee angle.

Cross‑Body Shoulder Stretch

Dose: 3 reps × 1 set, 30‑sec hold. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Stand/sit tall; keep shoulder blade down.

Start (what to think)

  • Bring arm across chest at shoulder height.

Move (direction & control)

  • Use other arm to gently pull closer (no shrugging).

Peak / End range

  • Hold where you feel stretch behind shoulder.

Where you should feel it

  • Posterior shoulder (back of shoulder).

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Shoulder hiking toward ear.
  • Pulling into pain/pinching.

Tricep Stretch + Neck Stretch (Upper Trap)

Dose: 3 reps × 1 set, 30‑sec hold each. Focus: controlled reps

Setup

  • Keep breathing slow; shoulders relaxed down.

Start (what to think)

  • Tricep: elbow points up; hand behind head.
  • Neck: ear toward shoulder; opposite arm reaching down.

Move (direction & control)

  • Gentle pull only — never force.

Peak / End range

  • Hold at mild‑moderate stretch; jaw relaxed.

Where you should feel it

  • Tricep/lat line + side of neck/upper trap.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Shrugging into the stretch.
  • Cranking the neck.

Cardio (pain‑free)

Dose: 15 min stationary bike + 15 min incline walk (comfortable pace & incline). Swimming optional. Focus: controlled reps

Video

  • No single perfect embed for this one — use the “Open video” buttons below.

Setup

  • Choose a load that keeps symptoms ≤ 3/10 during and settles within 24 hours.

Start (what to think)

  • Bike: saddle height so knee has slight bend at bottom stroke.

Move (direction & control)

  • Walk: shorter stride, steady cadence, relaxed shoulders.

Peak / End range

  • Stop immediately if sharp pain, catching, or giving‑way.

Where you should feel it

  • You should feel “worked” but not aggravated.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Pushing intensity too fast.
  • Ignoring symptom flare that lasts into next day.
